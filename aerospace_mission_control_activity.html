Mission Control: Aerospace Systems Challenge

Students act as a flight team preparing a lunar mission. They must pass four short mission stations: choose a vehicle, plan an orbit, survive reentry, and decode a communication problem. Every section is self-guided, auto-checked, and built to feel more like a challenge than a worksheet.

1. Pick the launch vehicle

Your spacecraft must carry scientific equipment to lunar orbit. Bigger rockets carry more mass, but cost more fuel. Choose the best vehicle for the mission goals below.

Goal: carry at least 21 tons to translunar injection while keeping the budget as low as possible.

What students practice

Constraint-based engineering thinking: meet the minimum requirement, then optimize under a tradeoff.

3. Survive reentry

A return capsule is approaching Earth. The reentry angle matters: too steep and it burns up, too shallow and it skips back into space.

Fast science connection

Apollo missions had to hit a narrow entry corridor too. Real spacecraft design often depends on staying inside these kinds of limits.

4. Decode the communication failure

Mission Control received a corrupted status message. Use the clues to determine the missing 4-digit launch code.

Clues
  1. The code uses four different digits.
  2. The first digit is the number of main forces on an aircraft in level flight.
  3. The second digit is one more than the first.
  4. The third digit is the number of stages in a simple water rocket: fill, pressurize, launch, coast.
  5. The last digit is the number of digits in the binary number 101.
In level flight, planes balance lift, weight, thrust, and drag.

Extension ideas
